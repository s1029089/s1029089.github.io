Sickle Cell Disease

A group of disorders that affects red blood cells causing them to transform into a sickle shape.

About

The sickle cell disease is an inherited red blood cell disorder and is obtained when both parents have the mutation due to it being found on a recessive allele. When only one parent carries the sickle cell mutation, their child will not inherit the sickle cell disease. The mutation that causes this is the haemoglobin-Beta gene found on chromosome 11.

This genetic disorder is the most common disease around the world with the United States alone having approximately 100,000 victims. This is mainly due to the fact that this disease can be found anywhere in the world although it is most prevalent among people with African ancestors.

Newborns are diagnosed through a blood test that checks for sickle cells (also known as haemoglobin S). If the diagnosis return positive, doctors will examine a sample of blood using a microscope to check for large numbers of sickled red blood cells.

Symptoms

The irregular red blood cells die much faster than normal leading to a shortage of red blood cells and can also block flow due to their shape. A number of symptoms form as a result of these problems.

Symptoms include:
  • Anaemia
  • Episodes of pain
  • Swelling of hands and feet
  • Frequent infections
  • Delayed growth or puberty
  • Vision problems

Treatments

Currently, a bone marrow transplant is the only cure for this disease and even so, is very rarely used. This is because only siblings who doesn't have the sickle cell disease can donate. Otherwise, the victim of the sickle cell disease risk developing the graft-veersus-host disease which is when the new stem cells don't recognise the victim's body and attack its tissues. Stem cell transplantation is effective as the bone marrow produces sickle blood cells and when replaced, it will start producing normal blood cells again. A person who undergoes this cure has to stay about 3 months in the hospital for recovery.

Because of these risks, most people seek treatments that reduces pain episodes and counter symptoms. Blood transfusion is one method to reduce complications as the donated blood fills in the missing healthy red blood cells. The last type of treatment is the comsumption of medications such as narcotic and vitamin supplements. Narcotic helps by relieving pain while dulling the senses although it can be addictive. Vitamin supplements provides extra vitamins to promote noraml body functions as well as growth and development.
